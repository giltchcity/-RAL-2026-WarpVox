\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

\subsection{Evaluation Protocol}

We evaluate 39 RGB-D loop-closure cases from 18 Kimera-Multi
sequences~\cite{tian23arxiv_kimeramultiexperiments} and 12 LiDAR cases from six
KITTI sequences~\cite{Geiger2013IJRR}. Every method reconstructs the same
temporal interval and is evaluated against the same ground truth (GT): a 5 m
tube around the official Kimera-Multi trajectory, or GT-registered KITTI points
within the mapper's 25 m ray range. Predictions outside the GT frame are
aligned by a RANSAC--Umeyama $\mathrm{Sim}(3)$ fitted only to timestamp-matched
trajectory positions; no mesh-to-GT registration is performed. Evaluation
replays frozen inputs offline to reproduce Stage 1 after surface extraction;
the deployed mapper retains the same bounded state during fusion.

\noindent\textbf{Baselines.}
Uncorrected TSDF uses the pre-correction trajectory. Open3D~\cite{zhou2018open3dmodernlibrary3d}
and Wavemap~\cite{reijgwart2023wavemap} replay corrected poses into an
independent TSDF and a wavelet occupancy map, respectively; Voxgraph
~\cite{reijgwart2020voxgraph} optimizes rigid TSDF submaps. Voxblox
reintegration~\cite{Oleynikova_2017} is the representation-matched reference.
WarpVox instead corrects the existing TSDF from retained sensor-frame support
and the pre/post-correction trajectories.

Precision and recall use bidirectional nearest-neighbor tests, and F@10/25/50
refer to thresholds of 0.10/0.25/0.50 m. All methods share each case's temporal
crop, spatial region, two-million-point sample, random seed, and thresholds.
The main TSDF experiments use 0.25 m voxels and 1.0 m truncation; Wavemap uses a
matched 0.25 m finest level. Core times are measured on an AMD Ryzen 7 9700X
with 64 GB RAM from in-memory inputs and exclude bag reading, disk I/O,
serialization, visualization, evaluation, and evaluation-only Marching Cubes.

\subsection{Event-Triggered Comparison with Kimera-PGMO}

Kimera-PGMO and WarpVox occupy different operating points. Kimera-PGMO
continuously maintains a MeshFrontend deformation graph, whereas WarpVox is
invoked after a global trajectory revision. We therefore compare their final
states on ten complete Kimera-Multi bags. Both methods receive the same
MeshFrontend pre-mesh extracted from a 5 cm Voxblox map and the same final
VIO-to-PGMO trajectory correction. Kimera-PGMO additionally exploits timestamped
graph associations (3 m spacing, three neighbors, and a 20 s horizon), while
WarpVox reconstructs support from the recorded depth and VIO poses.

For bags exhibiting variation across asynchronous replays, we use the complete
trajectory--mesh pair with the lowest ATE, selected independently of mesh
accuracy, and apply its trajectory correction to both methods. Kimera-PGMO
returns a corrected mesh, whereas WarpVox returns a 5 cm TSDF. Because the
native pre-mesh is not accompanied by its source TSDF volume, this comparison
uses mesh-only Stage 4 and therefore isolates surface correction rather than
source-support transfer.

One WarpVox update improves mean F@25/F@50 by 1.24/3.03 pp while using
47.02 s versus Kimera-PGMO's 435.97 s accumulated over all callbacks. It obtains
higher F@50 on eight of ten bags, with per-bag differences from $-0.44$ to
$+11.74$ pp. These results place WarpVox at a complementary operating point:
it approaches, and on average exceeds, the final geometric accuracy of
continuous mesh optimization while returning a reusable TSDF at substantially
lower cumulative cost; Kimera-PGMO retains the distinct benefit of intermediate
corrections and frontend temporal associations.

\subsection{Extension to Gaussian Primitives}

To test representation transfer, we apply the same targets to a
SplaTAM~\cite{keetha2024splatam} map while changing only Gaussian centers. On
one sequence, F@25/F@50 rises from 17.23/35.25 to 23.12/40.59 and depth L1
falls from 173.93 to 142.54 cm in approximately 21 s; a corrected-pose rebuild
obtains 23.25/41.01 and 141.37 cm in approximately 2 h. PSNR/MS-SSIM/LPIPS
improve from 11.96/0.512/0.430 to 12.59/0.526/0.411. This experiment isolates
the target mechanism: Gaussian splats~\cite{kerbl2023gaussians} still lack the
signed-distance and observed-free-space semantics that make TSDF/ESDF mapping
WarpVox's primary robotics setting.
