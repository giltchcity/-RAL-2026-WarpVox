\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

\noindent\textbf{Assumptions.}
Following the local-consistency assumption of
Voxgraph~\cite{reijgwart2020voxgraph}, we assume that TSDF fusion under the
drifted pre-correction trajectory remains locally valid: surface geometry and
connectivity are locally coherent although the map is globally inconsistent.
WarpVox corrects this global inconsistency after loop closure; severely noisy
or topologically corrupted pre-correction maps are outside its scope.

\subsection{Problem and Streaming State}

Let $T_i^- ,T_i^+\in SE(3)$ map the sensor frame $S_i$ to the
world frame before and after a trajectory correction.  The map built with
$\{T_i^-\}$ consists of a TSDF $\Phi^-$, its weight field $w^-$,
voxel size $s$, and truncation distance $\tau$.  Marching Cubes
(MC)~\cite{10.1145/37402.37422} extracts the source surface
$\mathcal M^-=(V^-,F)$.

During normal fusion, WarpVox maintains bounded, timestamped support records.
For an active source vertex $v_j^-$,
\begin{equation}
\mathcal O_j=\{(t_i,m_{ij},a_{ij},c_{ij})\},
\qquad |\mathcal O_j|\le n_{\max},
\label{eq:records}
\end{equation}
where $m_{ij}$ is a calibrated surface sample expressed in $S_i$,
$a_{ij}$ is its association weight, and $c_{ij}$ is its retained confidence.
The records are organized by acquisition time, not by trajectory row index.
The record contract admits a streaming realization: before MC extraction,
bounded samples are keyed by active TSDF support, and surface extraction
resolves them to the vertex-indexed form in Eq.~\eqref{eq:records} without
retaining the depth stream.  Given these records, WarpVox implements
the map-correction contract
\begin{equation}
(\Phi^-,w^-,\mathcal M^-,\{T_i^+\},\mathcal O)
\longmapsto \Phi^+,
\label{eq:contract}
\end{equation}
where the warped mesh is an intermediate representation and $\Phi^+$ is a
standard Voxblox volume.

\subsection{Observation Association and Surface Targets}

For RGB-D, pixel $\bar u=[u,v,1]^\top$ with measured z-depth $z_{ij}$
defines the invariant sensor-frame sample
\begin{equation}
m_{ij}=z_{ij}K^{-1}\bar u.
\label{eq:sensor_sample}
\end{equation}
The pre-correction pose projects the source vertex into frame $i$, with a
mesh BVH~\cite{10.1145/1189762.1206075} rejecting occluded projections.  Let
$e^g_{ij}=\|[T_i^-\widetilde m_{ij}]_{1:3}-v_j^-\|$ be the 3D
disagreement, $e^z_{ij}$ the projected-versus-measured depth residual, and
$g_{ij}=|n_j^\top r_{ij}|$ the viewing-angle factor.  We use
\begin{equation}
a_{ij}=
\exp\!\left(-\frac{e^g_{ij}}{\sigma_g}\right)
\exp\!\left(-\frac{|e^z_{ij}|}{\sigma_z}\right)
\frac{g_{ij}}{1+z^{\rm proj}_{ij}/z_0},
\label{eq:association_weight}
\end{equation}
subject to a depth-dependent geometric gate and a minimum weight.  LiDAR uses
the same construction with spherical projection and radial range.  This is a
geometric support association rather than exact voxel-integration provenance.

After loop closure, the timestamp selects the corrected pose. For a missing
interior pose, translation is interpolated linearly and rotation by bounded
quaternion interpolation~\cite{shoemake1985slerp}. The same local sample then
defines the corrected target
\begin{equation}
q_{ij}^+=\left[T_i^+\widetilde m_{ij}\right]_{1:3},
\qquad \widetilde m_{ij}=[m_{ij}^\top,1]^\top.
\label{eq:corrected_target}
\end{equation}
Equation~\eqref{eq:corrected_target} is exact when the static-scene
association, calibration, and corrected pose are valid. Multiple target
candidates are aggregated robustly. With
$\omega_{ij}=a_{ij}c_{ij}/(1+z_{ij}/z_t)$, first compute
$\bar q_j=\sum_i\omega_{ij}q_{ij}^+/\sum_i\omega_{ij}$ and retain
\begin{equation}
I_j=\left\{i:\|q_{ij}^+-\bar q_j\|
\le \max\!\left(\epsilon_t,
\kappa_t\operatorname*{median}_{k}\|q_{kj}^+-\bar q_j\|\right)\right\}.
\label{eq:target_inliers}
\end{equation}
When fewer than three candidates are available, all are retained.  The target
and its observable dispersion are then
\begin{equation}
v_j^*=\frac{\sum_{i\in I_j}\omega_{ij}q_{ij}^+}
                 {\sum_{i\in I_j}\omega_{ij}},
\qquad
\sigma_j^2=\frac{\sum_{i\in I_j}\omega_{ij}
\|q_{ij}^+-v_j^*\|^2}{\sum_{i\in I_j}\omega_{ij}}.
\label{eq:target}
\end{equation}
For any $r>0$, weighted Markov inequality bounds the retained weight outside
radius $r$ by $\sigma_j^2/r^2$.  This measures candidate agreement; it does
not certify a mutually consistent but incorrect association.

\subsection{One-Shot Surface Deformation}

Let $d_j=v_j^*-v_j^-$ denote an accepted control displacement.  A local
source-surface predictor suppresses isolated controls before deformation:
\begin{equation}
\widehat d_j=
\frac{\sum_{k\in\mathcal N_j}(\|v_j^--v_k^-\|^2+\varepsilon)^{-1}d_k}
     {\sum_{k\in\mathcal N_j}(\|v_j^--v_k^-\|^2+\varepsilon)^{-1}},
\qquad \delta_j=\|d_j-\widehat d_j\|.
\label{eq:control_filter}
\end{equation}
The neighbourhood is restricted to the same connected component when enough
support exists, and a median--MAD rule rejects large $\delta_j$.

To make the solve depend on surface complexity rather than raw frame count,
component-separated spatial clustering~\cite{rossignac1993vertexclustering}
selects QEM representatives~\cite{garland1997qem} to construct a proxy
$\mathcal P^-=(P^-,E_P)$. Accepted surface targets are aggregated on this proxy.
Robust local rigid consensus computes weighted orthogonal-Procrustes
fits~\cite{schoenemann1966procrustes} with bounded Welsch
influence~\cite{yao2020welsch}, supplies synthetic support, and replaces a grossly
inconsistent measured proxy target only when a strict component majority
supports the replacement.  The proxy deformation solves the cotangent
ARAP problem~\cite{ARAP_modeling:2007,10.1007/978-3-662-05105-4_2,libigl}
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\min_{P^+,\{R_a\in SO(3)\}}
&\sum_{(a,b)\in E_P}c_{ab}
\left\|(p_a^+-p_b^+)-R_a(p_a^--p_b^-)\right\|^2,\\
\text{s.t.}\quad &p_c^+=p_c^*,\qquad c\in\mathcal C_P .
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:arap}
\end{equation}
Thus the accepted proxy targets are hard positional constraints, while ARAP
interpolates the unconstrained surface.  WarpVox performs one
proxy solve, transfers displacement to $V^-$ by topology-local interpolation,
and assigns an unconstrained component the median motion of its nearest valid
controls.  A bounded harmonic repair pass~\cite{sorkine2004laplacian} smooths
only faces with extreme edge stretch, area expansion, or aspect ratio; fixed dimensionless distortion
checks~\cite{aigerman2013bounded} omit unresolved faces.  Hence
the retained source and warped surfaces satisfy
\begin{equation}
\bar{\mathcal M}^-=(\bar V^-,\bar F),\qquad
\mathcal M^+=(\bar V^+,\bar F),\qquad \bar F\subseteq F.
\label{eq:retained_topology}
\end{equation}

For a source point $x$ and its nearest retained control $c$, let $h$ be their
geodesic distance and $e_c$ the control-target error.  If the ideal and
estimated displacement fields are locally Lipschitz with constants $L_*$ and
$\widehat L$, the hard constraint at $c$ gives
\begin{equation}
\|\widehat f(x)-f^*(x)\|
\le e_c+(L_*+\widehat L)h.
\label{eq:conditional_bound}
\end{equation}
This decomposition makes the assumptions explicit: association and target
estimation determine $e_c$, while surface coverage controls $h$; the unknown
$e_c$ and $L_*$ prevent an unconditional error certificate.

\subsection{Source-Supported TSDF Reconstruction}

The warped surface defines the corrected zero set~\cite{sussman1999redistancing},
while the source TSDF
provides observed-space semantics.  For output voxel center $x$, a
closest-triangle query~\cite{10.1145/2601097.2601199} on $\mathcal M^+$
returns face $f$, closest point $q^+$, and barycentric coordinates $\beta$.
Shared face indices define a source/warped triangle-frame correspondence
motivated by deformation transfer~\cite{sumner2004deformationtransfer} and locate the corresponding source point
$q^-=\sum_{k\in f}\beta_k\bar v_k^-$.  With corresponding unit normals
$n_f^+$ and $n_f^-$, define
\begin{equation}
\eta=(x-q^+)^\top n_f^+,
\qquad x^-=q^-+\eta n_f^-.
\label{eq:inverse_map}
\end{equation}
When $\Phi^-$ is supported at $x^-$, WarpVox combines warped geometry with
source orientation and confidence:
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
|\Phi^+(x)|&=\min(\tau,\|x-q^+\|),\\
\operatorname{sgn}\Phi^+(x)&=
\operatorname{sgn}\!\left(\eta\,\partial_{n_f^-}\Phi^-(q^-)\right),\\
w^+(x)&=\widetilde w^-(x^-).
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:hybrid_tsdf}
\end{equation}
Writing $d(x)=\|x-q^+\|$, unsupported voxels receive only a finite extraction
band,
\begin{equation}
w_{\rm fb}(x)=
\begin{cases}
1, & d(x)\le0.5s,\\
(1.5s-d(x))/s, & 0.5s<d(x)\le1.5s,\\
0, & d(x)>1.5s,
\end{cases}
\label{eq:fallback_weight}
\end{equation}
and transferred support beyond this band must connect to it without adding a
new sign crossing.  The resulting $\Phi^+$ is serialized as a native Voxblox
TSDF; its MC mesh is used only for visualization and evaluation.

\subsection{Complexity and Scope}

Let $A$, $P$, and $X^+$ denote the numbers of active surface-support elements,
proxy vertices, and allocated output voxels.  For the streaming realization
above, the state and loop-time computation satisfy
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
S_{\rm stream}&=O(n_{\max}A),\\
C_{\rm corr}&=O(n_{\max}A)+C_{\rm ARAP}(P,E_P)+O(X^+).
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:complexity}
\end{equation}
The accumulated frame count $N$ does not occur: each active support retains at
most $n_{\max}$ records, and the remaining work follows the proxy and output
volume.  WarpVox therefore removes accumulated frame count from the correction
complexity.
