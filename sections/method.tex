\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

\noindent\textbf{Assumptions.}
Following the local-consistency assumption of
Voxgraph~\cite{reijgwart2020voxgraph}, we assume that TSDF fusion under the
drifted pre-correction trajectory remains locally valid: surface geometry and
connectivity are locally coherent although the map is globally inconsistent.
WarpVox corrects this global inconsistency after loop closure; severely noisy
or topologically corrupted pre-correction maps are outside its scope.

\subsection{Problem and Streaming State}

Let $T_i^- ,T_i^+\in SE(3)$ map sensor frame $S_i$ to the world frame before
and after trajectory correction.  The map built with $\{T_i^-\}$ consists of
a TSDF $\Phi^-$, its weight field $w^-$, voxel size $s$, and truncation
distance $\tau$.  Marching Cubes (MC)~\cite{10.1145/37402.37422} extracts the
source surface $\mathcal M^-=(V^-,F)$.

During normal fusion, WarpVox maintains a bounded cache of timestamped support
samples keyed by active TSDF cells.  After MC extraction, the records resolved
for an active source vertex $v_j^-$ are
\begin{equation}
\mathcal O_j=\{(t_i,m_{ij},a_{ij},\gamma_{ij})\},
\qquad |\mathcal O_j|\le n_{\max},
\label{eq:records}
\end{equation}
where $m_{ij}$ is a calibrated surface sample expressed in $S_i$,
$a_{ij}$ is its geometric association weight, and
$\gamma_{ij}\in[0,1]$ is its retained measurement confidence.  We write
$\mathcal O=\{\mathcal O_j\}_{j=1}^{|V^-|}$.  The records are organized by
acquisition time, not by trajectory row index.  Each extracted vertex queries
a fixed neighbouring-cell stencil, applies the geometric validation below,
and retains at most $n_{\max}$ samples, yielding Eq.~\eqref{eq:records}
without retaining the depth stream.  WarpVox then implements
\begin{equation}
(\Phi^-,w^-,\mathcal M^-,\{T_i^+\},\mathcal O)
\longmapsto (\Phi^+,w^+),
\label{eq:contract}
\end{equation}
where the warped mesh is an intermediate representation and
$(\Phi^+,w^+)$ is a standard Voxblox TSDF volume.

\subsection{Observation Association and Surface Targets}

For RGB-D, pixel $\bar u=[u,v,1]^\top$ with measured z-depth $z_{ij}$ defines
the invariant sensor-frame sample
\begin{equation}
m_{ij}=z_{ij}K^{-1}\bar u,
\qquad
\widetilde m_{ij}=[m_{ij}^\top,1]^\top.
\label{eq:sensor_sample}
\end{equation}
The pre-correction pose projects the source vertex into frame $i$.
Candidate observations are retained through depth consistency,
three-dimensional geometric agreement, and viewing-angle support.  Let
$n_j^-$ denote the source-vertex normal and $r_{ij}^-$ the unit viewing
direction, both expressed in the pre-correction world frame, and let
$z_{ij}^{\rm proj}$ be the projected depth of $v_j^-$.  We define
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
e^g_{ij}
&=\left\|[T_i^-\widetilde m_{ij}]_{1:3}-v_j^-\right\|,\\
e^z_{ij}
&=z_{ij}^{\rm proj}-z_{ij},\\
g_{ij}
&=\left|(n_j^-)^\top r_{ij}^-\right|.
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:association_residuals}
\end{equation}
The association weight is
\begin{equation}
a_{ij}=
\exp\!\left(-\frac{e^g_{ij}}{\sigma_g}\right)
\exp\!\left(-\frac{|e^z_{ij}|}{\sigma_z}\right)
\frac{g_{ij}}{1+z_{ij}^{\rm proj}/z_0},
\label{eq:association_weight}
\end{equation}
subject to a depth-dependent geometric gate and a minimum weight.  LiDAR uses
the same construction with spherical projection and radial range.  This is a
geometric support association rather than exact voxel-integration provenance.

After loop closure, each timestamp selects the corresponding corrected pose.
For a missing interior pose, translation is interpolated linearly and rotation
by SLERP over a bounded gap~\cite{shoemake1985slerp}.  The same invariant
sample defines the corrected target
\begin{equation}
q_{ij}^+=\left[T_i^+\widetilde m_{ij}\right]_{1:3}.
\label{eq:corrected_target}
\end{equation}
Equation~\eqref{eq:corrected_target} is exact when the static-scene
association, calibration, and corrected pose are valid.  Let $\mathcal J_j$
index the records in $\mathcal O_j$, and let $\rho_{ij}$ denote modality-native
range, with $\rho_{ij}=z_{ij}$ for RGB-D and
$\rho_{ij}=\|m_{ij}\|$ for LiDAR.  With
\begin{equation}
\omega_{ij}=
\frac{a_{ij}\gamma_{ij}}{1+\rho_{ij}/\rho_t},
\qquad
\bar q_j=
\frac{\sum_{i\in\mathcal J_j}\omega_{ij}q_{ij}^+}
     {\sum_{i\in\mathcal J_j}\omega_{ij}},
\label{eq:target_initial}
\end{equation}
we retain
\begin{equation}
I_j=\left\{i\in\mathcal J_j:
\|q_{ij}^+-\bar q_j\|
\le
\max\!\left(
\epsilon_t,
\kappa_t\operatorname*{median}_{k\in\mathcal J_j}
\|q_{kj}^+-\bar q_j\|
\right)\right\}.
\label{eq:target_inliers}
\end{equation}
When fewer than three candidates are available, $I_j=\mathcal J_j$.  The
accepted target and its observable dispersion are
\begin{equation}
v_j^*=
\frac{\sum_{i\in I_j}\omega_{ij}q_{ij}^+}
     {\sum_{i\in I_j}\omega_{ij}},
\qquad
\sigma_j^2=
\frac{\sum_{i\in I_j}\omega_{ij}\|q_{ij}^+-v_j^*\|^2}
     {\sum_{i\in I_j}\omega_{ij}}.
\label{eq:target}
\end{equation}
For any $r>0$, the fraction of retained weight outside radius $r$ satisfies
\begin{equation}
\frac{
\sum_{\substack{i\in I_j:\\ \|q_{ij}^+-v_j^*\|>r}}\omega_{ij}
}{
\sum_{i\in I_j}\omega_{ij}
}
\le \frac{\sigma_j^2}{r^2}.
\label{eq:target_markov}
\end{equation}
This bound measures agreement among retained candidates; it does not certify
an association that is mutually consistent but incorrect.

\subsection{One-Shot Surface Deformation}

Let $d_j=v_j^*-v_j^-$ denote an accepted control displacement.  A local
source-surface predictor suppresses isolated controls before deformation:
\begin{equation}
\widehat d_j=
\frac{\sum_{k\in\mathcal N_j}
(\|v_j^--v_k^-\|^2+\varepsilon)^{-1}d_k}
{\sum_{k\in\mathcal N_j}
(\|v_j^--v_k^-\|^2+\varepsilon)^{-1}},
\qquad
\delta_j=\|d_j-\widehat d_j\|.
\label{eq:control_filter}
\end{equation}
Here $\mathcal N_j$ contains accepted neighbouring controls other than $j$.
It is restricted to the same connected component when enough support exists,
and a median--MAD rule rejects large $\delta_j$.

To make the solve depend on surface complexity rather than raw frame count,
component-separated spatial clustering~\cite{rossignac1993vertexclustering}
constructs a proxy $\mathcal P^-=(P^-,E_P)$ and selects one QEM
representative~\cite{garland1997qem} per occupied cell.  Accepted surface
targets are aggregated on this proxy.  Robust local rigid consensus computes
weighted orthogonal-Procrustes fits~\cite{schoenemann1966procrustes} with
bounded Welsch influence~\cite{yao2020welsch}, supplies synthetic support,
and replaces a grossly inconsistent measured proxy target only when a strict
component majority supports the replacement.  The proxy deformation solves
the cotangent ARAP problem~\cite{ARAP_modeling:2007,
10.1007/978-3-662-05105-4_2,libigl}
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\min_{P^+,\{R_a\in SO(3)\}}
&\frac{1}{2}
\sum_{a=1}^{|P^-|}
\sum_{b\in\mathcal N_P(a)}
c_{ab}
\left\|
(p_a^+-p_b^+)-R_a(p_a^--p_b^-)
\right\|^2,\\
\text{s.t.}\quad
&p_c^+=p_c^*,\qquad c\in\mathcal C_P .
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:arap}
\end{equation}
Here $\mathcal N_P(a)$ is the one-ring proxy neighbourhood and
$c_{ab}=c_{ba}$ are the symmetric cotangent-derived weights used by the
solver; the factor $1/2$ removes double counting.  Thus accepted proxy targets
are hard positional constraints, while ARAP interpolates the unconstrained
surface.

WarpVox performs one proxy solve and transfers displacement to $V^-$ by
topology-local interpolation.  For each unconstrained connected component,
three gauge constraints receive the coordinate-wise median displacement of a
fixed number of nearest valid observation controls.  A bounded harmonic repair
pass~\cite{sorkine2004laplacian} extends the displacement field only over
vertices incident to faces with extreme edge stretch, area expansion, or
aspect ratio.  Fixed dimensionless distortion
checks~\cite{aigerman2013bounded} omit faces that remain unresolved.  Hence
the retained source and warped surfaces satisfy
\begin{equation}
\bar{\mathcal M}^-=(\bar V^-,\bar F),\qquad
\mathcal M^+=(\bar V^+,\bar F),\qquad
\bar F\subseteq F.
\label{eq:retained_topology}
\end{equation}

Let $\widehat f,f^*:\bar{\mathcal M}^-\rightarrow\mathbb R^3$ denote the
estimated and ideal displacement fields.  For a source point
$y\in\bar{\mathcal M}^-$, let $c$ be its nearest retained hard-control index,
let $h=d_{\bar{\mathcal M}^-}(y,\bar v_c^-)$ be their geodesic distance, and
define
$e_c=\|\widehat f(\bar v_c^-)-f^*(\bar v_c^-)\|$.
If $\widehat f$ and $f^*$ are locally Lipschitz with constants
$\widehat L$ and $L_*$, respectively, then
\begin{equation}
\|\widehat f(y)-f^*(y)\|
\le e_c+(L_*+\widehat L)h.
\label{eq:conditional_bound}
\end{equation}
Because the accepted target is imposed as a hard constraint,
$\widehat f(\bar v_c^-)=d_c$.  Thus association and target estimation determine
$e_c$, while surface coverage controls $h$; the unknown $e_c$ and $L_*$
prevent an unconditional error certificate.

\subsection{Source-Supported TSDF Reconstruction}

The warped surface defines the corrected zero set~\cite{sussman1999redistancing},
while the source TSDF provides observed-space semantics.  For output voxel
center $x$, a closest-triangle query~\cite{10.1145/2601097.2601199} on
$\mathcal M^+$ returns face $f$, closest point $q^+$, and barycentric
coordinates $\beta$.  Shared face indices define a source--warped
triangle-frame correspondence motivated by deformation
transfer~\cite{sumner2004deformationtransfer} and locate
\begin{equation}
q^-=\sum_{k\in f}\beta_k\bar v_k^-.
\label{eq:source_correspondence}
\end{equation}
Let
\begin{equation}
R_f^\pm=
\begin{bmatrix}
t_{f,1}^\pm & t_{f,2}^\pm & n_f^\pm
\end{bmatrix}\in SO(3)
\label{eq:triangle_frames}
\end{equation}
denote consistently oriented orthonormal frames of the corresponding source
and warped triangles.  The first tangent follows the same indexed triangle
edge in both faces, and
$t_{f,2}^\pm=n_f^\pm\times t_{f,1}^\pm$.  The output location is pulled back
to the source volume by
\begin{equation}
\Psi_f(x)=q^-+R_f^-(R_f^+)^\top(x-q^+),
\qquad x^-=\Psi_f(x).
\label{eq:inverse_map}
\end{equation}
This transport retains both tangential and normal offsets relative to the
corresponding triangles.  Let
\begin{equation}
d^+(x)=\min\!\left(\tau,\|x-q^+\|\right),
\qquad
\Omega_{\rm tr}=
\left\{x:\widetilde w^-(\Psi_f(x))>0\right\},
\label{eq:transferred_support}
\end{equation}
where $\widetilde\Phi^-$ and $\widetilde w^-$ denote trilinear samples of the
source distance and weight fields.

Let $\Omega_{\rm fb}$ denote unsupported interior interpolation holes that are
locally connected to $\Omega_{\rm tr}$.  It excludes voxels whose closest
surface feature belongs to a topological open boundary.  Their finite fallback
weight is
\begin{equation}
w_{\rm fb}(x)=
\begin{cases}
1, & \|x-q^+\|\le0.5s,\\
(1.5s-\|x-q^+\|)/s,
& 0.5s<\|x-q^+\|\le1.5s,\\
0, & \|x-q^+\|>1.5s.
\end{cases}
\label{eq:fallback_weight}
\end{equation}
Let $s_f^+(x)\in\{-1,+1\}$ denote the source-consistent side sign induced by
transporting the orientation of face $f$ from $R_f^-$ to $R_f^+$.  WarpVox
defines the active output distance by
\begin{equation}
\Phi^+(x)=
\begin{cases}
\operatorname{sgn}\!\left(\widetilde\Phi^-(\Psi_f(x))\right)d^+(x),
& x\in\Omega_{\rm tr},\\
s_f^+(x)d^+(x),
& x\in\Omega_{\rm fb},
\end{cases}
\label{eq:hybrid_tsdf}
\end{equation}
with weight
\begin{equation}
w^+(x)=
\begin{cases}
\widetilde w^-(\Psi_f(x)), & x\in\Omega_{\rm tr},\\
w_{\rm fb}(x), & x\in\Omega_{\rm fb},\\
0, & \text{otherwise}.
\end{cases}
\label{eq:hybrid_weight}
\end{equation}
Outside the active support, the stored distance is immaterial because its
weight is zero.  Any extension of transferred support is rejected if it
introduces a new sign crossing.  The resulting $(\Phi^+,w^+)$ is serialized
as a native Voxblox TSDF; its MC mesh is used only for visualization and
evaluation.

\subsection{Complexity and Scope}

Let $A$, $P$, and $X^+$ denote the numbers of active support cells, proxy
vertices, and allocated output voxels, respectively.  At fixed grid
resolution, MC produces only a constant number of surface elements per active
cell, so the extracted surface size is $O(A)$.  The additional streaming state
and loop-time correction satisfy
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
S_{\rm stream}&=O(n_{\max}A),\\
C_{\rm corr}
&=O(n_{\max}A)+C_{\rm ARAP}(P,E_P)+C_{\rm vol}(X^+,\bar F),
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:complexity}
\end{equation}
where $C_{\rm vol}$ is the accelerated closest-triangle reconstruction cost
over the allocated output volume.  The accumulated frame count $N$ does not
occur: each active support cell retains at most $n_{\max}$ records, and the
remaining work follows the proxy and output volume.  WarpVox therefore removes
accumulated frame count from the correction complexity.
