\section{Conclusion}
WarpVox converts an external trajectory correction into a Voxblox-compatible
TSDF by computing surface targets from bounded observation-support records,
performing one event-level surface deformation, and transferring source-volume
semantics to the corrected zero set. Its correction cost follows active
geometry rather than accumulated frame history, and the corrected volume
supports continued fusion. Across Kimera-Multi and KITTI it approaches full
reintegration at substantially lower core update cost. A separate ten-bag
final-state comparison improves mean F@50 by 3.03 percentage points over
Kimera-PGMO while one WarpVox update uses $9.27\times$ less core computation
than PGMO accumulated over all callbacks. The Gaussian-centre extension further
shows that observation-derived targets are not tied to voxel grids, while the
returned TSDF preserves the distance-map interface needed for continued
robotic mapping.
