\section{Conclusion}
WarpVox converts an external trajectory revision into a native Voxblox TSDF by
re-expressing bounded sensor-frame support, propagating the resulting targets
through one event-level surface deformation, and transferring source-volume
support to the corrected zero set. Its correction cost follows active geometry
rather than accumulated frame history. Across RGB-D and LiDAR benchmarks,
WarpVox remains close to full reintegration with mean core-time speedups of
$43.46\times$ and $3.23\times$, respectively. A separate ten-bag comparison
shows competitive final-state accuracy against continuous Kimera-PGMO mesh
optimization with a $9.27\times$ reduction in cumulative core time, while the
Gaussian-center extension indicates that the target construction is not tied
to voxel grids. The returned TSDF preserves the representation required for
continued robotic mapping.
