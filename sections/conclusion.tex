\section{Conclusion}
WarpVox converts an external trajectory correction into a reusable TSDF by
computing surface targets from bounded surface-support records, deforming the
retained surface once, and transferring source-volume semantics to the
corrected zero set.  Its correction cost follows active geometry rather than
accumulated frame history.  Across Kimera-Multi and KITTI, WarpVox is the
closest evaluated alternative to the representation-matched Voxblox
reintegration reference at every reported threshold, while requiring
substantially lower core update time.  A separate seven-checkpoint native-mesh
study shows higher mean accuracy and lower algorithm time than Kimera-PGMO on
that subset.  The Gaussian-centre extension further shows that
observation-derived targets are not tied to voxel grids, while the returned
TSDF preserves the distance-map interface needed for robotic mapping.
