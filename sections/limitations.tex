\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

WarpVox is a post-hoc map-correction method and therefore assumes that the
drifted pre-correction TSDF remains locally coherent. It introduces no new
surface adjacency and cannot recover missing or incorrectly fused topology;
rejecting invalid faces may create holes or separate components. Such failures
require reintegration from raw measurements. Its attainable accuracy is also
bounded by residual errors in the corrected trajectory. These limitations are
shared by methods that propagate pose corrections to an existing mesh rather
than rebuilding the map from sensor data.
