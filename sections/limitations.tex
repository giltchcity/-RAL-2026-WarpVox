\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

WarpVox is a post-hoc map-correction method and therefore assumes that the
drifted pre-correction TSDF remains locally coherent. It deforms existing
geometry but does not create, split, or merge surfaces; topology corrupted by
upstream fusion, or surfaces absent from the input mesh, require reintegration
from raw measurements. Its attainable accuracy is also bounded by residual
errors in the corrected trajectory. These limitations are shared by methods
that propagate pose corrections to an existing mesh rather than rebuilding the
map from sensor data.
