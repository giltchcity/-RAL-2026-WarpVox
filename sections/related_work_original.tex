\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\vspace{1mm}\noindent\textit{Volumetric SLAM and dense mapping.}
TSDF fusion~\cite{10.1145/3596711.3596726,newcombe2011kinectfusion} and sparse volume structures~\cite{10.1145/2508363.2508374,Oleynikova_2017,vizzo2022vdbfusion} enable incremental dense mapping from externally supplied poses. Their common assumption is that the input trajectory is fixed; none revises a persistent volume after a pose-graph back-end changes historical poses.

\vspace{1mm}\noindent\textit{Loop closure in dense SLAM.}
Kintinuous~\cite{whelan2015kintinuous} deforms accumulated point-cloud slices after loop closure rather than returning a corrected persistent TSDF for continued external fusion. ElasticFusion~\cite{whelan2016elasticfusion} frequently deforms a surfel map inside its own tracking system. FlashFusion~\cite{han2018flashfusion} and Voxgraph~\cite{reijgwart2020voxgraph} instead maintain rigid submaps, leaving spatially varying intra-submap errors unresolved. WarpVox receives an external trajectory correction, deforms a single extracted surface with observation-derived targets, and reconstructs one corrected TSDF.

\vspace{1mm}\noindent\textit{Non-rigid deformation for 3D reconstruction.}
ARAP~\cite{ARAP_modeling:2007} and embedded deformation~\cite{10.1145/1276377.1276478} preserve local shape while satisfying sparse controls. DynamicFusion~\cite{7298631}, KillingFusion~\cite{slavcheva2017killingfusion}, and VolumeDeform~\cite{innmann2016volumedeform} estimate continuous non-rigid scene motion. Our static-map setting instead receives a discrete trajectory correction; corrected poses generate the controls, and a single event-level QEM-proxy ARAP optimization propagates them.

\vspace{1mm}\noindent\textit{Neural and Gaussian maps.}
Neural SLAM systems~\cite{sucar2021imap,zhu2022niceslam,cui2023ngelslam} optimize implicit scene representations; Loopy-SLAM~\cite{liso2024loopyslam} re-optimizes them after loop closure, while Neural Graph Map~\cite{bruns2024neuralgraphmap} anchors local fields to pose-graph nodes. SplaTAM~\cite{keetha2024splatam} maps with 3D Gaussians~\cite{kerbl2023gaussians}, and LoopSplat~\cite{zhu2025loopsplat} aligns Gaussian submaps. These representations target rendering and do not natively encode the signed distance and observed free space needed for obstacle-distance mapping. WarpVox therefore targets TSDF/ESDF pipelines; its Gaussian experiment only tests whether observation-derived targets transfer beyond voxel grids.
