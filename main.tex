\begin{abstract}
Loop closure retroactively changes sensor poses after their range observations have been fused, leaving the TSDF inconsistent with the corrected trajectory. Full reintegration restores consistency but scales with frame history. WarpVox instead retains bounded, timestamped observation support during fusion. After correction, it re-expresses this support with the new poses, robustly estimates surface targets, propagates them through a single event-level proxy deformation, and transfers the warped zero set and source-volume support into a Voxblox-compatible TSDF. Online correction therefore requires no historical range-frame replay and scales with active geometry. Across 39 Kimera-Multi and 12 KITTI checkpoints at 0.25 m, WarpVox differs from reintegration by 0.06 and 0.32 percentage points at F@25, while reducing core update time from 109.25 to 2.43 s and from 15.73 to 4.89 s. On Kimera-Multi, it obtains 48.40 F@50 versus 48.67. A ten-bag final-state comparison improves mean F@50 by 3.03 percentage points over Kimera-PGMO, while one WarpVox update requires $9.27\times$ lower core time than PGMO accumulated over all callbacks. A geometry-only SplaTAM extension reaches 40.59 F@50 versus 41.01 for a corrected-pose rebuild in approximately 21 s rather than 2 h. WarpVox introduces no new surface adjacency and cannot recover missing or topologically corrupted geometry.
\end{abstract}
