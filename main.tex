\begin{abstract}
Loop closure retroactively changes sensor poses after their range observations have been fused, leaving a TSDF inconsistent with the corrected trajectory. Full reintegration restores consistency but revisits the entire frame history. WarpVox instead retains bounded, timestamped sensor-frame support during fusion. When a trajectory revision arrives, WarpVox re-expresses these samples with corrected poses to form surface targets, propagates the targets through a single event-level proxy deformation, and reconstructs a Voxblox-compatible TSDF whose zero set follows the warped surface while observed support is inherited from the source volume. The correction stage therefore requires no historical range-frame replay and scales with active geometry. Across 39 Kimera-Multi and 12 KITTI checkpoints at 0.25 m, WarpVox remains within 0.05 and 0.32 percentage points of reintegration at F@25 while reducing mean core time from 109.25 to 2.51 s and from 15.73 to 4.87 s. In a ten-bag final-state comparison, WarpVox improves mean F@50 by 3.03 points over Kimera-PGMO with a $9.27\times$ reduction in cumulative core time. A geometry-only SplaTAM extension reaches 40.59 F@50 versus 41.01 for a corrected-pose rebuild in approximately 21 s rather than 2 h. WarpVox cannot recover geometry or topology absent from the source map.
\end{abstract}
